My Dice

My Dice, also known as "Langur Burja" in Nepal and "Crown and Anchor" in England and other parts of the world, is a traditional gambling game widely played in India. Unlike conventional dice games, My Dice utilizes a unique six-faced dice, each face adorned with distinct symbols â€“ "Lion," "Horse," "Panda," "Elephant," "Deer," and "Monkey."

The game is typically played by tossing the special dice onto a flat surface, and participants place bets on which symbol will land facing up once the dice come to a rest. The excitement lies in the unpredictability of the outcome, making it a thrilling game of chance.
Originating from the cultural fabric of India, My Dice has been a popular pastime for generations. Its simple yet engaging gameplay, coupled with the symbolic significance of the animal images, adds an extra layer of enjoyment for players.

  • Six dice are rolled, and the player places a bet on one of the six symbols.
  • If no dice show the symbol the player had bet on, then the player loses the bet to the dealer.
  • If one or more dice show the symbol the player had bet on, then the player receives the bet amount multiplied by the number of times the symbol was rolled plus the original bet amount.
